About Hameeda Hossain

Hameeda Hossain is one of Bangladesh's influential human rights defenders — a legal scholar, writer, activist, and prominent figure of the women's rights movement in the country. Her work spans law, journalism, activism, and institution-building across more than five decades.

She co-founded Ain o Salish Kendra (ASK), Bangladesh's leading legal aid and human rights organisation, and served as its executive director for many years. She has been a consistent voice for democratic rights, minority protections, and gender justice in both national and international forums.

As a writer and editor, she shaped public discourse through the magazines and publications she built, her extensive journalism, and her essays on law, politics, and society. She has contributed forewords and chapters to numerous books on South Asian women's rights and constitutional law.

This collective gathers the breadth of her published work, personal writings, and historical documents in one place, so that her contribution to public life is preserved and accessible to researchers, students, and anyone interested in the history of human rights in Bangladesh.
